Problem 19.2.1.4.

Assume \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) \(c\text{,}\) and \(d\) are known constants and generalize the results in Problem 19.2.1.3 a bit further by showing that if
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{(ax+b)(cx+d)} = \frac{A}{ax+b}+\frac{B}{cx+d} \end{equation*}
then
\begin{align*} A=\frac{a}{ad-bc}\amp{}\amp{}\text{ and }\amp{}\amp{} B=\frac{-c}{ad-bc} \end{align*}
