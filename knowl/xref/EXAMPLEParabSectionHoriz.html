Example 21.2.0.10.

Compute the area of the following region bounded by the curves
\begin{align*} y\amp{}=0 \text{ (the }x-\text{axis)}\\ y\amp{}=x+2\\ y\amp{}=\sqrt{4-x}. \end{align*}
🔗
A diagram of the essential features of this problem is given below. In the future you will be expected to provide your own. In all of mathematics there is nothing quite as useful as an aid to both understanding and calculation as a visual representation, however crude, of the problem you are working on. Always sketch your problem and label it appropriately.
🔗
Clearly we need to accumulate the areas of differential rectangles \(x=-2\) to \(x=4\text{,}\) so it would seem that the definite integral to be evaluated is
\begin{equation*} \int_{x=-2}^{4}f(x)\dx{x} \end{equation*}
where the graph of \(f(x) \) is curve in red in our diagram.
🔗
This is a correct understanding of the problem, but the actual computation of the integral requires a little more. In particular, notice that our function comes in two parts:
\begin{equation*} f(x) = \begin{cases} x+2 \amp{} \text{ if } -2\le{}x\lt0\\ \sqrt{4-x} \amp{} \text{ if }0\le{}x. \end{cases} \end{equation*}
That is, the nature of our function changes at \(x=0\) and we need to account for the change when we compute the integral.
🔗

Aside: Piecewise Functions.

Be sure you are clear on this point: We do not have two functions, we have a single function in two parts. Which part we use depends on whether the \(x\) is greater than or less than zero.
🔗
In particular, the differential rectangles all have the form \((x+2)\dx{x} \) when \(x\le{}0\) and the form \(\sqrt{4-x}\dx{x}\) when \(x\ge{}0\text{.}\) Thus we have
\begin{align*} \int_{x=-2}^{4}f(x)\dx{x}\amp{}= \int_{x=-2}^{0}f(x)\dx{x} + \int_{x=0}^{4}f(x)\dx{x}\\ \amp{}= \underbrace{\int_{x=-2}^{0}(x+2) \dx{x}}_{=2} + \underbrace{\int_{x=0}^{4}\sqrt{4-x}\dx{x}}_{=\frac{16}{3}}.\\ \amp{}=\frac{22}{3.} \end{align*}
We need to split the integral up in this case because the differential rectangles do not have the same form across the entire interval \(\left[0, 4\right]\text{.}\)
