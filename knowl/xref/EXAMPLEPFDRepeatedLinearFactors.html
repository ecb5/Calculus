Example A.12.0.5. The PFD With Repeated Linear Factors.

Let’s finish up the decomposition of \(\frac{x}{(x-1)^2(x+2)^2}\) from part (b) of Problem A.12.0.4. From the HCUM You should have found that \(B=\frac19\) and \(D=\frac{-2}{9}\) so that
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \frac{x}{(x-1)^2(x+2)^2}= \frac{A}{x-1} + \frac{\frac19}{(x-1)^2} +\frac{C}{x+2} -\frac{\frac29}{(x+2)^2} \end{equation*}
When we reached this point in [provisional cross-reference: prov] (see part (b) of [provisional cross-reference: prov]) our next step was to simply choose a value for \(x\text{.}\) We could do that here as well but because we now have two unknowns we’d have to choose two distinct values for \(x\) and generate a system of two coupled equations to solve. We’ve already seen that there are more efficient methods for computing the PFD than solving a system of equations.
🔗
Instead we’ll isolate all of the terms with our unknowns on one side of the equation, thus:
\begin{equation} \displaystyle \frac{x}{(x-1)^2(x+2)^2} - \frac{\frac19}{(x-1)^2} +\frac{\frac29}{(x+2)^2} = \frac{A}{x-1} +\frac{C}{x+2}\tag{A.9} \end{equation}
🔗
It is clear that the right side of equation (A.9) is the PFD of some rational function whose denominator is \((x-1)(x+2)\text{.}\) That is, if we were asked to begin the process of computing the PFD of
\begin{equation*} \frac{P(x)}{(x-1)(x+2)} \end{equation*}
for some polynomial \(P(x)\) then our first step would be to write down the right side of equation (A.9). Therefore it must be that
\begin{equation} \displaystyle \frac{x}{(x-1)^2(x+2)^2} - \frac{\frac19}{(x-1)^2} +\frac{\frac29}{(x+2)^2} = \frac{P(x)}{(x-1)(x+2)}\tag{A.10} \end{equation}
for some polynomial \(P(x)\text{,}\) and if we can find that polynomial we can compute \(A\) and \(C\) by the HCUM.
🔗
To find \(P(x)\text{,}\) we first clear the fraction on the right leaving,
\begin{equation} \frac{x}{(x-1)(x+2)} - \frac19 \left( \frac{x+2}{x-1} \right) +\frac29 \left( \frac{x-1}{x+2} \right) = P(x)\tag{A.11} \end{equation}
It is tempting to find a common denominator and add the three fractions on the left together. Since we have to end up with the polynomial \(P(x)\) it must be that once the addition is done there will be a factor in the top that will cancel with the common denominator.
🔗
That will work. Give it a try if you want. Good luck.
🔗
But we know that \(P(x)\) is a polynomial and we can make very effective use of that knowledge as follows.
🔗
If we perform this division in the second term on the left we get
\begin{equation*} \frac{x+2}{x-1} = 1 +\frac{3}{x-1}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Similarly if we perform the division in the third term we get \(\frac{x-1}{x+2}= 1-\frac{3}{x+2}\text{.}\) Putting these back into equation (A.11) we have
\begin{equation*} \frac{x}{(x-1)(x+2)} - \frac19 \left( 1 +\frac{3}{x-1} \right) +\frac29 \left( 1-\frac{3}{x+2} \right) = P(x) \end{equation*}
or
\begin{equation} \underbrace{\left( \frac{x}{(x-1)(x+2)} -\frac{\frac13}{x-1} -\frac{\frac23}{x+2}\right)}_{=0} + \frac19 = P(x)\text{.}\tag{A.12} \end{equation}
Since \(P(x)\) must be a polynomial it follows that the expression in the parentheses — everything that is not a polynomial — must be equal to zero, as we’ve indicated above.
🔗
Thus \(P(x)=\frac19{}\text{.}\) To finish the decomposition we place this in to equation (A.10) and observe that
\begin{equation*} \frac{\frac19 }{(x-1)(x+2)} = \frac{A}{x-1}+\frac{C}{x+2} \end{equation*}
and from the HCUM we see that \(A=\frac{1}{27} \) and \(C=-\frac{1}{27}\text{.}\)
🔗
Having found all of the constants we conclude from equation (A.9) that
\begin{equation*} \frac{x}{(x-1)^2(x+2)^2}= \frac{\frac{1}{27}}{x-1} + \frac{\frac19 }{(x-1)^2} -\frac{\frac{1}{27}}{x+2} -\frac{\frac29 }{(x+2)^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
🔗
Naturally, it isn’t always that simple. If the degree of any (or all) of the numerators in equation (A.12) was not less than it’s denominator then we’d have had to compute the division in order to recover the polynomial part of each. But then the sum of the remainders would necessarily be zero so they can be ignored just as we ignored \(\frac{x}{(x-1)(x+2)} -\frac{\frac13}{x-1} -\frac{\frac23}{x+2}\) above.
