Theorem A.12.0.2. The Partial Fraction Decomposition (PFD).

If \(P(x)\text{,}\) \(Q(x)\text{,}\) and \(R(x)\) are polynomials having no non–trivial common factors with \(\deg(P)\lt\deg(Q)+ \deg(R)\) then there exist polynomials \(A(x)\) and \(B(x)\text{,}\) with \(\deg(A)\lt\deg(Q)\) and \(\deg(B)\lt\deg(R)\text{,}\) such that
\begin{equation*} \frac{P(x)}{Q(x)\cdot{}R(x)} = \frac{A(x)}{Q(x)}+\frac{B(x)}{R(x)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
