The expressions \(\frac12\left(e^x+e^{-x}\right)\) and \(\frac12\left(e^x-e^{-x}\right)\) arise frequently in many scientific and engineering problems and have been named the hyberbolic cosine and hyberbolic sine. They are denoted \(\cosh(x)\) and \(\sinh(x)\text{,}\) respectively. These names break with the longstanding mathematical tradition of giving important functions three letter names. Why do you suppose we would do that?
