That is, if \(\frac{1}{(x-a)(x-b)}= \frac{A}{x-a}+\frac{B}{x-b}\) then
\begin{equation*} A=\eval{\frac{1}{(\text{covered})(x-b)}}{x}{a}=\frac{1}{a-b}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Similarly
\begin{equation*} B=\eval{\frac{1}{(x-a)(\text{covered})}}{x}{b}=\frac{1}{b-a}\text{.} \end{equation*}
This trick is known as Heaviside’s Cover–up Method (HCUM) and it will work for any number of distinct linear factors in the denomiator. Heaviside’s Method is the simplest way we know to compute the coefficients of the PFD of the rational functions we’ve seen so far. Which method seems simplest?
