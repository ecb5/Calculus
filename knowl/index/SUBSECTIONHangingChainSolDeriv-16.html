Problem 19.1.4.3. The Hanging Chain Problem.

(a)

Recall that the lowest point on the chain is at the point \((0,0)\text{.}\) Use IVP (19.23) to show that \(z\left(0\right)=0\) so that \(C=0\text{.}\)
🔗
🔗

(b)

Use equation (19.24) to show that \(z\) must satisfy
\begin{equation*} \sqrt{1+z^2}=e^{\frac{w}{H}x}-z \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗

(c)

Square both sides of the result in part (b) to show that \(z\) must satisfy
\begin{equation*} 2e^{\frac{w}{H}x}z={\left(e^{\frac{w}{H}x}\right)}^2-1 \end{equation*}
and ultimately
\begin{equation*} z=\frac{1}{2}\left(e^{\frac{w}{H}x}-e^{-\frac{w}{H}x}\right) \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗

(d)

So, we’ve reduced the original catenary problem, IVP (19.23) to solving the IVP
\begin{align} \dfdx{y}{x}=\frac{1}{2}\left(e^{\frac{w}{H}x}-e^{-\frac{w}{H}x}\right), \amp{}\amp{}y\left(0\right)=0.\tag{19.25} \end{align}
🔗
Separate the variables and integrate both sides of IVP (19.25) to derive (finally) the solution of equation (18.16).
