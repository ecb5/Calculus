Definition 9.5.4.4. Possible Optimal Transition Points (POTPs).

The possible optimum transition points of a function, \(O(x)\text{,}\) come in two categories:
  1. Any point, \(x=a\text{,}\) where \(\dfdxat{O}{x}{a}=0\) is a possible optimal transition point.
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Any point, \(x=a\text{,}\) where \(\dfdxat{O}{x}{a}\) is not defined is a possible optimal transition point.
