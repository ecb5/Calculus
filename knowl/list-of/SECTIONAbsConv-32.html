Problem 25.4.0.5.

(a)

Show that if \(a_n\ge 0\) and \(\sum^{\infty }_{n=0}{a_n}\) converges, then any rearrangement \(\sum^{\infty }_{n=0}{b_n}\) converges and \(\sum^{\infty }_{n=0}{b_n}\le \sum^{\infty }_{n=0}{a_n}\)
🔗
Hint.
The sequence of partial sums for \(\sum^{\infty }_{n=0}{b_n}\) is an increasing sequence bounded above by \(\sum^{\infty }_{n=0}{a_n}\text{.}\)]
🔗
🔗
🔗

(b)

Show that in fact \(\sum^{\infty }_{n=0}{b_n}=\sum^{\infty }_{n=0}{a_n}\text{.}\)
🔗
Hint.
By part a, we know that \(\sum^{\infty }_{n=0}{b_n}\) converges. Notice that \(\sum^{\infty }_{n=0}{a_n}\) is a rearrangement of this.
