Problem 21.7.0.34.

Let’s assume that \(b=1\) and focus on the curved part of the trumpet generated by revolving \(y=\frac{1}{x}, 1\le x\infty \) about the \(x\) axis. We know that the surface area of an infinitely small piece of this is given by \(2\pi y\dx{s}\)
🔗

(a)

Putting things in terms of \(x\text{,}\) show that the surface area is given by the improper integral
\begin{equation*} 2\pi \int^{\infty }_{x=1}{\frac{1}{x}\sqrt{1+\frac{1}{x^4}} \dx{x}} \end{equation*}
Computing this will not be easy, but notice that this integral is greater than \(2\pi \int_{ x=1}\dx{x}\) (Why?) Use this x fact to show that the surface area is infinite.
🔗
🔗

(b)

Putting the original integral in terms of \(y\text{,}\) show that the surface area is given by
\begin{equation*} 2\pi \int^1_{y=0}{\sqrt{\frac{1}{y^2}+y^2}\dx{y}} \end{equation*}
Notice that this is still an improper integral (Why?). Again, computing this will not be easy so try a trick like you did in part a to show that this is infinite.
🔗
