Problem 18.2.0.2.

🔗

(a)

Integrate both sides of equation (18.12) and then show that \(y\) must satisfy
\begin{equation} \ln \abs{y}+C_1=\frac{x^2}{2}+C_2\tag{18.13} \end{equation}
where \(C_1\text{,}\)and \(C_2\) are arbitrary constants.
🔗
🔗

(b)

Explain why the formula
\begin{equation} \ln\abs{y} = \frac{x^2}{2}+C\tag{18.14} \end{equation}
is equivalent to equation (18.13) .
🔗
🔗

(c)

Show that
\begin{equation*} y=\pm \left(e^C\right)e^{\frac{x^2}{2}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗
Since \(C\) is an arbitrary constant, it follows that \(e^C\) is also an arbitrary constant. (Why?) For simplicity we will set \(A=e^C\text{,}\) so \(y=\pm Ae^{\frac{x^2}{2}}\) provides the general solution for the original differential equation.
