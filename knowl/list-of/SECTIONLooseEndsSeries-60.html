Theorem 25.7.0.14. Cauchy’s Form of the Remainder for Taylor Series.

Suppose \(f\) is a function which is continuous on an interval containing \(a\) and \(x\text{.}\) Then
\begin{align*} f\left(x\right)=f\left(a\right)+\frac{f^\prime \left(a\right)}{1!}{\left(x-a\right)}^1\amp{}+\frac{f''\left(a\right)}{2!}{\left(x-a\right)}^2+\dots +\frac{f^{\left(N\right)}\left(a\right)}{N!}{\left(x-a\right)}^N\\ +\amp{}\frac{f^{\left(N+1\right)}\left(c\right)}{N!}{\left(x-c\right)}^n(x-a) \end{align*}
where \(c\) is some number between \(a\) and \(x\text{.}\)
