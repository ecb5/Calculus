Problem 24.2.0.5.

Suppose \(f(x)\) is continuous on \([a,b]\) and \(F\left(x\right)\) is any antiderivative of \(f(x)\) which is continuous on \([a,b]\text{.}\) Show that
\begin{equation*} \int^b_{t=a}{f\left(t\right)\dx{t}}=F\left(b\right)-F(a) \end{equation*}
🔗
Hint.
We have two antiderivatives of \(f(x)\) which are continuous on \([a,b]\text{:}\) \(F(x)\) and \(\int^x_{t=a}{f\left(t\right)\dx{t}}\text{.}\) These must differ by a constant, so
\begin{equation*} F\left(x\right)=\int^x_{t=a}{f\left(t\right)\dx{t}}+C \end{equation*}
What must this constant be?
