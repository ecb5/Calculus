Example 19.2.3.4.

Our solution to the problem in Example 19.2.3.2 probably appeared to be fairly complex but that is only because we needed to carefully explain our reasoning at each step. Since we don’t need to do that anymore this example will probably seem much simpler, even though it is actually a little bit more complex.
🔗
Consider the function
\begin{gather*} R(x) = \frac{-2x^4+9x^3-7x^2+20x-12}{x^3(x-2)^2}. \end{gather*}
Its decomposition has the form
\begin{align*} R(x)\amp{} = \frac{-2x^4+9x^3-7x^2+20x-12}{x^3(x-2)^2}= \frac{A}{x}+\frac{B}{x^2}+\frac{C}{x^3}+\frac{D}{x-2}+\frac{E}{(x-2)^2}. \end{align*}
From Heaviside’s Method we see immediately that
\begin{gather*} C=\eval{\frac{-2x^4+9x^3-7x^2+20x-12}{(\text{covered})(x-2)^2}}{x}{0}=-3 \end{gather*}
and
\begin{gather*} E=\eval{\frac{-2x^4+9x^3-7x^2+20x-12}{x^3(\text{covered})}}{x}{2}=5. \end{gather*}
🔗
Thus we have
\begin{align*} R(x) \amp{}= \frac{-2x^4+9x^3-7x^2+20x-12}{x^3(x-2)^2}\\ \amp{}=\frac{A}{x}+\frac{B}{x^2}+\textcolor{red}{\frac{-3}{x^3}}+\frac{D}{x-2}+\textcolor{red}{\frac{5}{(x-2)^2}} \end{align*}
or
\begin{equation} \frac{-2x^4+9x^3-7x^2+20x-12}{x^3(x-2)^2} +\textcolor{red}{\frac{3}{x^3}} -\textcolor{red}{\frac{5}{(x-2)^2}}= \frac{A}{x}+\frac{B}{x^2}+\frac{D}{x-2}.\tag{19.59} \end{equation}
but the right side of equation (19.59) is the PFD of \(\frac{P(x)}{x^2(x-2)}\text{,}\) where \(P(x)\) is a polynomial to be determined next.
🔗
We now have
\begin{gather*} \frac{-2x^4+9x^3-7x^2+20x-12}{x^3(x-2)^2} +\frac{3}{x^3} -\frac{5}{(x-2)^2}=\frac{P(x)}{x^2(x-2)}. \end{gather*}
Clearing the fractions we have
\begin{gather*} \frac{-2x^4+9x^3-7x^2+20x-12}{x(x-2)} +3\left(\frac{x-2}{x}\right) -5\left(\frac{x^2}{x-2}\right)=P(x). \end{gather*}
Performing the indicated divisions and ignoring any non-polynomials gives
\begin{align*} P(x) \amp{}= {\left[\frac{-2x^4+9x^3-7x^2+20x-12}{x(x-2)}\right]}\amp{}\RedApproach{-2x^2+5x+3}\\ \amp{}\ \ \ + {\left[3\left(\frac{x-2}{x}\right)\right]}\amp{}\RedApproach{3}\\ \amp{} \ \ \ -{\left[5\left(\frac{x^2}{x-2}\right)\right]}\amp{}\RedApproach{5(x+2)}\\ \amp{}=-2x^2-4. \end{align*}
🔗
Thus we have
\begin{gather*} \frac{-2x^2-4}{x^2(x-2)}=\frac{A}{x}+\frac{B}{x^2}+\frac{D}{x-2} \end{gather*}
from which Heaviside’s Method gives us
\begin{align*} B=\frac{-4}{-2}=2\amp{}\amp{}\text{and}\amp{}\amp{}D=\frac{-2(4)-4}{4}=-3. \end{align*}
Solving for \(A\text{,}\) performing the indicated divisions, and ignoring any non–polynomial terms we see that
\begin{align*} A\amp{}={\left[\frac{-2x^2-4}{x(x-2)}\right]}\amp{}\RedApproach{-2}\\ \amp{}-{\left[\frac{2}{x}\right]}\amp{}\RedApproach{0}\\ \amp{}+{\left[\frac{3}{x-2}\right]}\amp{}\RedApproach{3}\\ \amp{}=1. \end{align*}
🔗
Thus we have the decomposition
\begin{gather*} R(x) = \frac{1}{x}+\frac{2}{x^2}-\frac{3}{x^3}-\frac{3}{x-2}+\frac{5}{(x-2)^2} \end{gather*}
