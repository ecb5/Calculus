Problem 26.1.0.21.

Using the transformations \(x=r\cos \left(\theta \right)\text{,}\)\(y=r\sin (\theta )\text{,}\) \(x^2+y^2=r^2 \text{,}\) show that the polar equation \(r=2\cos (\theta)\)transforms into the cartesian equation \(x^2+y^2=x\) which then becomes the equation \(\left(x-1\right)^2+y^2=1\text{.}\)
🔗
Hint.
Multiply both sides of the equation \(r=2{\cos \left(\theta \right)}\) by \(r\text{.}\)
