Problem 9.7.3.3.

You have a piece of wire \(100\) inches long.
🔗

(a)

Find the length of each piece of the wire that yields the least total area bounded by the pieces when:
🔗
  1. You cut the wire into two pieces and bend each piece to form a square.
    🔗
    🔗
  2. You cut the wire into two pieces and bend each piece to form a circle.
    🔗
    🔗
  3. You cut the wire into two pieces and bend one piece to form a square and the second to form a circle.
    🔗
    🔗
  4. You cut the wire into two pieces and bend one piece to form a rectangle with one side three times as long as the other. Then bend the second piece to form a square.
    🔗
    🔗
  5. You cut the wire into three pieces and form two squares and one circle.
    🔗
    🔗
🔗
🔗

(b)

Repeat each problem in part (a), but this time find the length of each piece of the wire that yields the most total area bounded by the pieces.
