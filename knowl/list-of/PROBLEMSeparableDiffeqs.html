Problem 18.2.0.3.

Use Table 18.1.0.4 (if necessary) to confirm that the function \(y\) shown solves the separable differential equation (SDE). Then find the most general solution (MGS).
🔗

(a)

SDE: \(\dfdx{y}{x} = (1+x)(1+y)\text{,}\) MGS: \(y= (1+x)e^{\frac{x^2}{2}+x}\)
🔗
🔗

(b)

SDE: \(\dfdx{y}{x} = 9x^2y^2\text{,}\) MGS: \(y=\frac{-1}{3x^3}\)
🔗
🔗

(c)

SDE: \(\dfdx{y}{x}=x^3\inverse{y}\text{,}\) MGS:\(y=\frac{x^2}{2}\)
🔗
🔗

(d)

SDE: \(\dx{y}+2xe^{-y}\dx{x} =0 \text{,}\) MGS:\(y=-2\ln(x)\)
🔗
🔗

(e)

SDE: \(\dfdx{y}{x}=x^3\sec(y)\text{,}\) MGS: \(y=\frac{x^3}{\sqrt{1-\left( {x^4}/{4}\right)^2 }}\)
🔗
🔗

(f)

SDE: \((1+t^2)\dx{y}=(1+y^2)\dx{t} \text{,}\) MGS: \(y=t\)
