Theorem 25.7.0.3.

If there exists a number \(B\) such that \(\abs{f^{\left(N+1\right)}\left(t\right)}\le B\) for all nonnegative integers \(N\) and for all \(t\) on an interval containing \(a\) and \(x\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \limit{N}{ \infty}{ \left(\frac{1}{N!}\int^x_{t=a}{f^{\left(N+1\right)}\left(t\right){\left(x-t\right)}^Ndt}\right)}=0 \end{equation*}
and so
\begin{equation*} f\left(x\right)=\sum^{\infty }_{n=0}{\frac{f^{\left(n\right)}\left(a\right)}{n!}{\left(x-a\right)}^n} \end{equation*}
