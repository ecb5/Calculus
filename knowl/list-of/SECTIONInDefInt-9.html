Integration and Initial Value Problems.

Because we stated Theorem 20.2.0.3 using the language and symbolism of integration you may not recognize it, but you’ve seen this sort of thing before. To see what we mean let \(F\) be the function defined in Theorem 20.2.0.3. Notice that \(F(a)=0\text{.}\) Since
\begin{gather*} \dfdx{F}{x}= F^\prime{}(x) = f(x) \end{gather*}
we see that \(F(x)\) is the solution of the Initial Value Problem:
\begin{align*} \dfdx{F}{x} = f(x),\amp{}\amp{}F(a)=0. \end{align*}
