Problem 5.8.0.12.

The ideal gas law (Boyle’s Law) states that the pressure \(P\) (in Pascals), volume \(V\) (in cubic meters), and temperature \(T\) (in degrees Kelvin) of an ideal gas are related by the formula \(PV=kNT\) where \(N\) is the number of gas molecules and \(k\) is the Boltzmann constant. What this says is that for a fixed amount of gas, if the volume is held fixed, then the pressure is proportional to the temperature and if the temperature is held fixed, then the pressure is inversely proportional to the volume.
🔗

(a)

Assuming that we have an enclosed gas, and that \(N\) is held constant. Find an equation that relates \(\dx{P}\text{,}\) \(\dx{V}\text{,}\) and \(\dx{T}\text{,}\) and use this to find \(\dfdx{T}{t}\) in terms of \(\dfdx{P}{t}\) and \(\dfdx{V}{t}\text{.}\)
