Example A.13.1.6.

Let \(a,b\) be distinct real numbers. Then
\begin{align*} \frac{1}{{\left(x-a\right)}^2\left(x-b\right)}\amp{}=\frac{1}{x-a}\left(\frac{1}{\left(x-a\right)\left(x-b\right)}\right)\\ \amp{} =\frac{1}{x-a}\left(\frac{1}{a-b}\left(\frac{1}{x-a}-\frac{1}{x-b}\right)\right)\\ \amp{} =\frac{1}{a-b}\left(\frac{1}{{\left(x-a\right)}^2}-\frac{1}{\left(x-a\right)\left(x-b\right)}\right)\\ \amp{} =\frac{1}{a-b}\left(\frac{1}{{\left(x-a\right)}^2}-\frac{1}{a-b}\left(\frac{1}{x-a}-\frac{1}{x-b}\right)\right)\\ \amp{} =\frac{\frac{1}{a-b}}{{\left(x-a\right)}^2}-\frac{\frac{1}{{\left(a-b\right)}^2}}{x-a}+\frac{\frac{1}{{\left(a-b\right)}^2}}{x-b} \end{align*}
🔗
Again, don’t try to memorize such a formula. The purpose of this is to show that such a PFD exists. There are more efficient ways to compute the coefficients as you have seen.
