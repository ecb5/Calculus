Problem 25.7.0.19.

(a)

Show that the sum of the triangular areas lying above the curve \(y=\frac{1}{x}\) is given by -->
\begin{equation*} \sum^{N-1}_{n=1}{\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)}-{\ln (N)} \end{equation*}
and use this to show that \(\left(\sum^{N-1}_{n=1}{\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)}-{\ln \left(N\right)}\right)\) is an increasing sequence.
🔗
🔗

(b)

Show geometrically that all of the triangular pieces will fit without overlapping inside the first rectangle and use this to show that -->
\begin{equation*} \sum^{N-1}_{n=1}{\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)}-{\ln \left(N\right)}\le 1 \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗

(c)

By our completeness axiom, this sequence must converge to some number. Show that \(\left(\sum^N_{n=1}{\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)}-{\ln \left(N\right)}\right)\) converges to the same number (which Euler denoted by \(y\)).
