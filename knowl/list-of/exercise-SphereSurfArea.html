Problem 21.5.0.11. The Surface Area of a Sphere.

🔗

(a)

Use figure 21.5.0.11 with the equation \(y=\sqrt{a^2-x^2}\text{,}\) \(-a\le x\le a\) to show that the surface area of a sphere of radius \(a\) is \(4\pi a^2\) as Archimedes claimed.
🔗
🔗

(b)

Use the parameterization
\begin{equation} x=\cos (\theta )\tag{21.6} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} y=a\sin( \theta )\tag{21.7} \end{equation}
obtain the same answer as part (a).
