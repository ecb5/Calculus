Theorem 25.3.0.3. The Integral Test.

Suppose \(f(x)\) is nonnegative decreasing (actually nonincreasing) integrable function defined on \([1,\infty )\text{.}\) If
\begin{equation*} \int^{\infty }_{x=1}{f(x)}dx\lt{}\infty \end{equation*}
then
\begin{equation*} \sum^{\infty }_{n=1}{f(n)}\lt{}\infty \end{equation*}
🔗
If
\begin{equation*} \int^{\infty }_{x=1}{f\left(x\right)dx}=\infty \end{equation*}
then
\begin{equation*} \sum^{\infty }_{n=1}{f\left(n\right)}=\infty \end{equation*}
