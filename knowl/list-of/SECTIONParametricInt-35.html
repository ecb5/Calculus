Problem 26.1.0.12.

Consider the circle whose equation is given by \(x^2+y^2=R^2\text{.}\) Show that the area of this circle is given by
\begin{gather*} \int^R_{x=-R}{2y\dx{x}}=2\int^R_{x=-R}{\sqrt{R^2-x^2}\dx{x}} \end{gather*}
and use the appropriate trigonometric substitution to compute this integral to obtain \(\pi{}R^2\text{.}\)
