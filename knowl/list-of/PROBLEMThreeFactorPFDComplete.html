Problem 19.2.1.5.

The formulas for \(A\) and \(B\) derived in Problem 19.2.1.4 represent a complete solution of the problem of decomposing a rational function with \(1\) in the numerator and two linear factors in the denominator. It was straightforward, if a bit tedious. But suppose the denominator has three distinct factors:
\begin{equation} \frac{1}{\left(x-a\right)\left(x-b\right)\left(x-c\right)}=\frac{A}{x-a}+\frac{B}{x-b}+\frac{C}{x-c}\text{.}\tag{19.38} \end{equation}
Clear the fractions, gather the coefficients of \(x^2\text{,}\) \(x\text{,}\) and the constant term to show that \(A\text{,}\) \(B\text{,}\) and \(C\) must satisfy the equations:
\begin{align*} A+B+C\amp{}=1\\ (b+c)A+(a+c)B+(a+b)C\amp{}=0\\ (bc)A+(ac)B+(ab)C\amp{}=0 \end{align*}
