Problem A.12.0.8.

In Example A.12.0.5 we stated without justification that the sum of the reduced rational functions \(\frac{x}{(x-1)(x+2)} -\frac{\frac13}{x-1} -\frac{\frac23}{x+2}\) would be another reduced rational function but is that really true? This problem explores that question.
🔗
Suppose that \(\frac{A(x)}{B(x)}\) and \(\frac{C(x)}{D(x)}\text{,}\) are reduced rational functions. Show that the sum
\begin{equation*} \frac{A(x)}{B(x)}+\frac{C(x)}{D(x)}= \frac{A(x)\cdot D(x)+C(x)\cdot B(x)}{B(x)\cdot D(x)} \end{equation*}
is also a reduced rational function.
