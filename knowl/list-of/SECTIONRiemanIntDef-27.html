Problem 24.1.0.6.

(a)

Suppose \(F(x)\) is Riemann integrable on \([a,b]\) with \(F\left(x\right)\ge 0\) on \([a,b]\text{.}\) Show that
\begin{equation*} \int^b_{x=a}{F(x)}\dx{x}\ge 0 \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗

(b)

Suppose \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) are Riemann integrable on \([a,b]\) with \(f\left(x\right)\le g(x)\) on \([a,b]\text{.}\) Show that
\begin{equation*} \int^b_{x=a}{f\left(x\right)}\dx{x}\le \int^b_{x=a}{g\left(x\right)}\dx{x} \end{equation*}
🔗
Hint.
Consider \(F\left(x\right)=g\left(x\right)-f(x)\) and apply the result of Part a.
