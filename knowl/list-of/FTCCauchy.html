Theorem 24.2.0.3. The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Cauchy).

Suppose \(f(x)\) is continuous on \([a,b]\) and define \(G(x)\) on \([a,b]\) by
\begin{equation*} G\left(x\right)=\int^x_{t=a}{f\left(t\right)\dx{t}} \end{equation*}
Then \(G(x)\) is continuous on \([a,b]\) and is differentiable on \((a,b)\) with \(G^\prime \left(x\right)=f(x)\)
