Problem 19.1.3.8. Quadratic Drag Conclusion.

In Cornerstone Problem 19.1.3.1 we saw that the problem of determining the velocity \(v\) of an object falling subject to quadratic drag came down to solving this equation
\begin{equation} \int\sec (\theta )\dx{\theta} =\sqrt{\frac{Rg}{M}}t+C\tag{19.17} \end{equation}
where we had made the substitution \(\sqrt{\frac{R}{Mg}}v=\sin (\theta )\text{.}\)
🔗

(a)

Integrate the left side of equation (19.17) and unwind the substitution \(\sqrt{\frac{R}{Mg}}v=\sin (\theta )\) to show that
\begin{equation} \ln\sqrt{\frac{1+\sqrt{\frac{R}{Mg}}v}{1-\sqrt{\frac{R}{Mg}}v}} = \sqrt{\frac{Rg}{M}}t +C.\tag{19.18} \end{equation}
🔗
🔗

(b)

Use the initial condition \(v(0)=0 \) to show that \(C=1\text{.}\)
🔗
🔗

(c)

Exponentiate both sides of equation (19.18) and solve for \(v\) to show that
\begin{equation*} v=\sqrt{\frac{Mg}{R}}\left[\frac{1-e^{-2\sqrt{\frac{Rg}{M}}t}}{1+e^{-2\sqrt{\frac{Rg}{M}}t}}\right] \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗

(d)

Compute \(\limit{t}{\infty}{v}\) to obtain the terminal velocity. Compare this with the terminal velocity we found in Linear Drag and Terminal Velocity: Sand Falling in Water.
