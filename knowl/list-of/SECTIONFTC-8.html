Problem 24.2.0.2.

In the statement of the MVT for Integrals, we assumed \(a\le b\) and considered the integral \(\int^b_{x=a}{f\left(x\right)\dx{x}}\text{.}\) Show it also works for \(\int^a_{x=b}{f\left(x\right)\dx{x}}\text{.}\) That is, show that there is a \(c\in [a,b]\) with
\begin{equation*} \int^a_{x=b}{f\left(x\right)\dx{x}}=f(c)(a-b) \end{equation*}
🔗
Hint.
Notice that \(\int^a_{x=b}{f\left(x\right)\dx{x}}=-\int^b_{x=a}{f\left(x\right)dx}\text{.}\) By what we have shown, there is a \(c\in [a,b]\) with
\begin{equation*} \int^b_{x=a}{f\left(x\right)\dx{x}}=f\left(c\right)\left(b-a\right) \end{equation*}
You should be able to finish it from here.
